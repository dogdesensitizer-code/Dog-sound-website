How to Use Calm Dog Thunder

  1. Choose a session length (5–30 minutes).
  2. Set master volume low (10–25%).
  3. Use Low and High frequency sliders to reduce rumbles and cracks; start around 75% reduction and ease back over time.
  4. Tap Start Session and reward calm behavior.

Frequently Asked Questions

Will this help my dog's thunderstorm anxiety?

Many owners find gradual sound exposure helpful. Start at low volume and short sessions, and consult your veterinarian for personalized guidance.

What do “Low” and “High” frequency sliders do?

Thunder has two main characters—deep low-frequency rumbles and sharp high-frequency cracks. These sliders let you reduce each range independently to match your dog’s natural hearing sensitivity. Begin with larger reductions (e.g., 75%) and only ease back as your dog remains relaxed.

Do I need special speakers?

Not required, but clearer speakers or a soundbar help reproduce thunder (especially lows). Keep the volume comfortable and avoid very loud subwoofers.

Does this replace professional training?

No. It is an educational tool, not a substitute for veterinary or professional training advice.
